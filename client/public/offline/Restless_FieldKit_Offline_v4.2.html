Tool 01 · Sale To Be Made

A Sale To Be Made.

ENTR-454 / T01Sale To Be Made
Protocol A

03 / 30

Purpose. Before the math, before the script, before the ladder — answer six questions out loud. What you sell, to whom, in what steps, how long, what you fear, and what changes if you do this well. If any answer is fuzzy, the week is already behind.

AThe Six Prompts — Answered Out Loud
Say These Before You Touch The Phone, The Door, or The Keyboard
01 / What

What is my sale to be made?

One clearly dosed energy-plus-hydration stick for crews that already buy energy drinks — traded for a real-shift trial and an honest yes/no.

02 / Who

To whom?

P1 responders & military-adjacent sponsors · P2 gym / combat / responder-fitness · P3 healthcare, label-first.

03 / Steps

Six key steps to the sale

Name the room — earn the intro — ship a coded micro-trial — crew tries on a real shift — honest yes/no — route: re-order, station bag, or clean exit.

04 / How Long

Typical time to close

3–8 weeks from first named contact to first paid re-order. Responder / MWR longer; coach / gym shortest (sometimes one WOD).

05 / Fear

Biggest fear

Shipping a kit into a room that never tries it — budget spent, no signal back. Second: a healthcare pitch that overclaims and burns the lane.

06 / Upside

If I do this well

A named crew, coach, or unit becomes a repeat buyer + community reference. One station referral unlocks the next two. Peer adoption, not polish, moves the book.

BWhen The Buyer Names One

CShelf Rules
Concede · Compare · Never Attack
  • Walk the aisle first. Name every SKU before you say one of ours.
  • Concede one thing out loud. Not certified yet, not mass-shelved yet. Buyers trust reps who don't pretend.
  • Compare on one axis. Caffeine dose, form factor, OR hydration. Never all three at once.
  • Never attack an incumbent. The buyer probably knows someone there.
  • Offer side-by-side, not swap. "Let the shelf decide, not the rep."
  • If buyer won't drop coffee, don't argue. Ask: "Where does coffee fall short — if anywhere?"
  • No health claims to beat a competitor. Ever.
If All Else Fails. Drop the comparison. Ask: "What would you need to see in a small trial to even consider a second option?" Listen, code the trial, follow up by the date you set.

ARules 01–05 · What You Ship
Mechanics Of Every Trial
  • 01 · Code before product. Every ship gets a unique Shopify code generated before the box leaves. No code — no ship. No exceptions.

Language Discipline
  • 06 · No hero claims. Never "purpose-built," "proven," "well-regarded," or any number you can't point to in the fact bank.
  • 07 · Cite the label, not the pitch deck. When a buyer wants a number, read it off the Supplement Facts panel. That's the source of truth.
  • 08 · "I don't know" is a winning answer. Followed by a deadline: "— let me get you the exact figure by Thursday."
  • 09 · The founder's story is the backdrop, not the pitch. Bring it out once, briefly, when a buyer asks why you. Then return to their world.
  • 10 · Walk away clean. A real "no" is a gift. Thank them, log it, re-queue to 90 days. Pressure kills the next conversation too.

5 Trial Mechanics — How to Prove Fit Without Overclaiming

Use when: Buyer asks "Has anyone else tried this?" and you do not have an attributable, written case study to cite.
Who the trial is for: A named champion (captain, MWR coordinator, charge nurse, head coach) with a crew of 12–30 and one real shift to test on.
What it tests: A specific shift or moment — the hour-3 fade on a 24-hour, the ruck drawdown, the 2 p.m. floor slump.
What the rep watches for: Did the champion finish a stick? Did the crew ask where to get more? Did the champion text back unprompted?
Counts as a reorder signal: "Send more" within 7 days, or 8+ thumbs-up from a 12-person crew.
Counts as a clean no: Champion silent at the agreed follow-up date, or "It's fine, but we'll stick with what we have." Code it. Move.

6 Science Brief REP-ONLY — DO NOT QUOTE TO HEALTHCARE BUYERS

Audience: Internal coaching only. Useful for rep credibility on analytical objections; not a buyer-facing talk track and not a clinical claim.
Use when: Rep is preparing for a science-literate buyer and wants ingredient-level orientation, NOT to recite to a healthcare buyer.
Coaching frame: Caffeine + 240 mg L-theanine has published ingredient-level research on attention and stress modulation. Rhodiola rosea is studied for acute-stress adaptation. Prebiotic fiber is included as part of the formula architecture; do not describe absorption effects unless the evidence pack supports it. Magnesium bisglycinate is included as a bioavailable magnesium form; do not claim jitter reduction. Electrolytes (Na / K / Mg) are part of the hydration formula. None of this is a clinical outcome claim — the label is still the source of truth, and none of this language is for buyer-facing use.
